4-Inch Black Forest Cake with ½ Cup Flour (Bad cake)

Ingredients:

  • All-purpose flour: ½ cup (60 grams)
  • Cocoa powder: 1 tablespoon (since the flour is not reduced)
  • Baking powder: ½ teaspoon
  • Baking soda: ⅛ teaspoon
  • Jaggery powder (or sugar): ¼ cup (50 grams)
  • Oil: 2 tablespoons (neutral-flavored oil like vegetable oil or melted butter)
  • Buttermilk from curd: ¼ cup + 1 tablespoon
  • To make buttermilk: 2 tablespoons curd (yogurt) mixed with 2 tablespoons water or milk.
  • Vanilla extract: ½ teaspoon
  • Vinegar or lemon juice: ¼ teaspoon (for boosting the reaction with baking soda)
  • Pinch of salt: Optional, enhances flavor

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Air Fryer:
  2. Preheat your air fryer to 150°C (300°F).

  3. Grease a 4-inch round cake pan (2-inch height) with butter or oil, and line the bottom with parchment paper.

  4. Prepare Buttermilk from Curd:

  5. In a small bowl, whisk 2 tablespoons of curd (yogurt) with 2 tablespoons of water or milk to thin it out. This will serve as your buttermilk substitute.

  6. Mix the Dry Ingredients:

  7. In a bowl, sift together:
    • ½ cup all-purpose flour
    • 1 tablespoon cocoa powder
    • Baking powder (½ teaspoon)
    • Baking soda (⅛ teaspoon)
    • A pinch of salt (optional).

  8. Set aside.

  9. Mix the Wet Ingredients:

  10. In another bowl, whisk together:

    • ¼ cup jaggery powder (or sugar)
    • 2 tablespoons oil (or melted butter)
    • Buttermilk from curd (¼ cup + 1 tablespoon) that you prepared earlier.
    • Vanilla extract (½ teaspoon).
    • ¼ teaspoon vinegar or lemon juice to further enhance the reaction with baking soda.

  11. Combine Wet and Dry:

  12. Slowly add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring gently until just combined. Be careful not to overmix the batter, as it can make the cake dense.

  13. Bake in the Air Fryer:

  14. Pour the batter into the prepared 4-inch cake pan.
  15. Bake in the air fryer at 150°C (300°F) for 18-22 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  16. Check around the 18-minute mark to avoid overbaking.

  17. Cool the Cake:

  18. Once done, let the cake cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.

Optional Step:

  • Simple Syrup: If you want extra softness, brush the cake with a little simple syrup (equal parts water and sugar, boiled and cooled) after it comes out of the oven.

Changes in this Recipe:

  • We kept the ½ cup of flour and adjusted the cocoa powder to 1 tablespoon.
  • Baking powder is ½ teaspoon to give the cake enough lift and softness.
  • Buttermilk from curd keeps the cake moist and soft.

This modified version with ½ cup of flour should give you a nice, soft Black Forest Cake while maintaining balance between the ingredients.
